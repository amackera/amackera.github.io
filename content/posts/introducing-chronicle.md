I have a lot of recurring meetings. 1:1s with my manager, team syncs,
project check-ins. Every time I sit down for one, I'm trying to
remember what we talked about last time. Did I follow up on that
thing? What did we decide about the rollout? I end up scribbling notes
in some personal doc that the other person never sees, or more often,
I just wing it.

So I built [Chronicle](https://chronicle.work).

Chronicle gives you a shared, running history for each recurring
conversation. You create a chronicle for a relationship (a 1:1, a
team sync, whatever) and it becomes the place where what happened
lives. Both people contribute. Both people can look back.

The whole point is that the history belongs to the relationship, not
to one person's notebook. Next time you sit down for a meeting, you
open the chronicle and you have context. What you discussed. What's
still open. What you said you'd do.

It's simple on purpose. No AI summaries guessing what mattered. No
integrations pulling in noise from other tools. Just a chronological
record of what was said and decided, written by the people who were
there.

It's free. [Give it a try](https://chronicle.work) if you're tired
of starting every meeting from scratch.
